Step 2 · What was found
Searchable items · tick what visitors can find

Scenes and hotspots open reliably, so they're on by default. Photos, albums and videos are off because 3DVista's player usually can't open them from a script — in most tours they're shown through a hotspot pop-up or the menu, with no way for an outside script to open them on their own (so they'd appear in search but do nothing).
If you still want one in the search: tick it here — and in 3DVista give the hotspot that opens it a clear tooltip (e.g. “Open photo album”). Searching that wording then finds the hotspot and opens the pop-up — the tour's own way of showing it. (Tick a type only if it actually opens when you test the finished tour.)

✎ Names & search keywords — optional, but very useful
Each element has two optional boxes. You never lose the original name — it stays searchable either way.
  • Display name — a friendlier label to show and search instead of a vague one. e.g. rename “01” → “Kitchen counter”, or “Image” → “Floor plan”.
  • Extra search words — other words a visitor might type to find this item, separated by commas. The element still opens; these just help people find it. e.g. for a scene named “Entrance”, add: front door, main door, lobby, foyer, start → now searching any of those lands on Entrance.
💡 Tip: think of how a first-time visitor might describe the place in their own words, and add those. Works offline — the words are saved right inside your tour.

Step 3 · Get your search-enabled tour

Option A · Export the whole tour as a ready-to-publish .zip simplest

  • Easiest: unzip it, upload that folder, done — no editing at all.
  • Safe: your original tour folder is never touched — this only creates a new zip.
  • Heavy on memory: it rebuilds the entire tour (all media) in your browser. A very large tour (several GB) may be slow, or fail on a low-RAM computer.

👉 Choose this for normal-sized tours (up to roughly 1 GB).

Option B · Get just the two small files best for very large tours
⬇ vtsearch.js ⬇ patched index.htm
  • Tiny & safe on size: it never reads your media — no size limit, no memory worries, however heavy the tour is.
  • You install them yourself (two short steps below).
⚠ Installing it yourself — protect your original files:
  1. Copy vtsearch.js into your tour folder, right next to index.htm. This is a brand-new file, so it won't overwrite anything.
  2. The downloaded index.htm will REPLACE the one in your tour folder. Before you overwrite it, make a backup copy of your original index.htm (e.g. rename it index-original.htm) so you can always go back.
  3. Prefer not to replace it? Keep your own index.htm and just add this single line right before </body>: 
    <script src="vtsearch.js?v=1"></script>

👉 Choose this if the tour is very large, or if Option A is slow or fails.

Either option: nothing is uploaded anywhere — it all runs in your browser, and the finished search works the same online or offline. Re-running this tool later just makes a newer vtsearch.js; safe to overwrite the old one.

Finished with this tour? Clear everything and start over with a different one. Anything you already downloaded stays safely on your computer.
